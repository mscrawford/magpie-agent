## Overview

`vm_carbon_stock` is the central interface variable linking land carbon accounting (Module 52, realization `normal_dec17`) to GHG policy costs (Module 56, realization `price_aug22`). The variable is declared in Module 56's `declarations.gms:34` and populated by the land modules during optimization; Module 52 then reads it to compute CO2 emissions, which feed back into Module 56's cost equations.

---

## Step 1: Where vm_carbon_stock is declared and populated

`vm_carbon_stock(j, land, c_pools, stockType)` is declared in Module 56 (`declarations.gms:34`), not Module 52. Its dimensions are:
- `j`: simulation cells
- `land`: land types (crop, past, primforest, secdforest, urban, other, plantations)
- `c_pools`: carbon pools (vegc, litc, soilc)
- `stockType`: stock type (primarily "actual")

The variable is **populated during optimization by the land modules** (Modules 30, 31, 32, 34, 35, 58), each computing carbon stocks for its respective land type based on area decisions and the carbon densities provided by Module 52. Module 52 itself does not compute `vm_carbon_stock` — it reads it.

The companion parameter `pcm_carbon_stock(j, land, c_pools, stockType)` holds the **previous timestep's** carbon stock. Module 56 initializes `pcm_carbon_stock` in `preloop.gms:10-11` and then stores the current `vm_carbon_stock` as next-timestep's `pcm_carbon_stock` at the end of each solve. This is the recursive temporal accounting mechanism.

---

## Step 2: Module 52's upstream role — supplying carbon densities

Module 52 does not write `vm_carbon_stock` directly. Instead, it supplies the carbon density parameters that the land modules use to compute `vm_carbon_stock`:

**Base densities from LPJmL** (`input.gms:16-20`):
- `fm_carbon_density(t_all, j, land, c_pools)` — LPJmL-derived densities (tC/ha) for all land types and carbon pools, loaded from `lpj_carbon_stocks.cs3`. Climate scenario handling (`c52_carbon_scenario`) can freeze these at 1995 values (`nocc`) or after a threshold year (`nocc_hist`) (`input.gms:22-23`).

**Age-class-specific densities**, computed in `start.gms` (before optimization each timestep):

For vegetation carbon (vegc), the Chapman-Richards macro `m_growth_vegc(S, A, k, m, ac)` (defined at `core/macros.gms:18`) is applied:
```
m_growth_vegc = S + (A - S) * (1 - exp(-k * (ac * 5)))^m
```

Three parameters are written:
- `pm_carbon_density_plantation_ac(t_all, j, ac, ag_pools)` — `start.gms:17` (vegc) and `start.gms:20` (litc); vegc overwritten by growing-stock calibration in `preloop.gms` when `s52_growingstock_calib = 1` (default)
- `pm_carbon_density_secdforest_ac(t_all, j, ac, ag_pools)` — `start.gms:28` (vegc) and `start.gms:31` (litc); vegc likewise overwritten by calibration
- `pm_carbon_density_other_ac(t_all, j, ac, ag_pools)` — `start.gms:48` (vegc) and `start.gms:51` (litc); NOT calibrated (no FRA target for other land)

For litter carbon (litc), a linear-to-equilibrium macro `m_growth_litc_soilc(start, end, ac)` (`core/macros.gms:20`) interpolates linearly over 20 years (IPCC assumption), then holds constant. Soil carbon (soilc) is NOT age-class-specific — it is read directly from LPJmL static input.

Growth parameters `k` and `m` are climate-class-weighted: `sum(clcl, pm_climate_class(j,clcl) * f52_growth_par(clcl, par, forest_type))` (`start.gms:17`), using `f52_growth_par.csv` (`input.gms:40`) and climate classification from Module 45 (`pm_climate_class`).

**Growing-stock calibration** (`preloop.gms:1-118`, active when `s52_growingstock_calib = 1`): A regional bisection (25 iterations) adjusts `k` so that area-weighted growing stock matches FAO FRA 2025 targets (`f52_fra_nrf_gs.cs4`, `f52_fra_pla_gs.cs4`). After calibration, `pm_carbon_density_secdforest_ac` and `pm_carbon_density_plantation_ac` (vegc pool only) are overwritten with the calibrated growth curves (`preloop.gms:71-73` for secdforest, `preloop.gms:114-116` for plantations). Uncalibrated copies (`pm_carbon_density_secdforest_ac_uncalib`, `pm_carbon_density_plantation_ac_uncalib`) are preserved at `start.gms:43-44` for new-establishment contexts (Module 32 afforestation/NDC, Module 29 tree cover).

These density parameters feed into Modules 32 and 35, which use them when computing their contributions to `vm_carbon_stock` during the solve.

## Step 3: Module 52's emission equation — reading vm_carbon_stock

During optimization, Module 52 uses `vm_carbon_stock` (current) and `pcm_carbon_stock` (previous) in its single equation:

**Equation `q52_emis_co2_actual`** (`equations.gms:16-19`):

```gams
q52_emis_co2_actual(i2, emis_oneoff) ..
  vm_emissions_reg(i2, emis_oneoff, "co2_c") =e=
    sum((cell(i2,j2), emis_land(emis_oneoff, land, c_pools)),
      (pcm_carbon_stock(j2, land, c_pools, "actual")
       - vm_carbon_stock(j2, land, c_pools, "actual"))
      / m_timestep_length);
```

**Interpretation:**
- The stock difference `pcm_carbon_stock - vm_carbon_stock` is positive when carbon is lost (emission) and negative when carbon is gained (sequestration).
- Division by `m_timestep_length` (defined at `core/macros.gms:51`) converts the stock change (mio. tC) to an annual rate (Tg C/yr; units are consistent because 1 mio. tC = 1 Tg C).
- The summation ranges over all cells `j2` in region `i2` and all land-type/carbon-pool combinations in the `emis_land` mapping set.
- Output: `vm_emissions_reg(i, emis_oneoff, "co2_c")` — regional CO2-C emissions by source (Tg C/yr). This variable is declared in Module 56 (`declarations.gms`) and written by Module 52.

The `emis_oneoff` sources include `crop_vegc`, `crop_litc`, `crop_soilc`, `past_vegc`, `past_litc`, `past_soilc`, `forestry_vegc`, `forestry_litc`, `forestry_soilc`, `primforest_vegc`, `primforest_litc`, `primforest_soilc`, `secdforest_vegc`, `secdforest_litc`, `secdforest_soilc`, `urban_vegc`, `urban_litc`, `urban_soilc`, `other_vegc`, `other_litc`, `other_soilc` (`core/sets.gms:314-318`).

## Step 4: Module 56 — two pathways for CO2 into GHG costs

Module 56 (realization `price_aug22`) handles CO2 from land-use change through **two related but distinct pathways**:

### Pathway A: vm_emissions_reg through q56_emis_pricing

For annually recurring emissions, Module 56 routes `vm_emissions_reg` directly into pricing:

**Equation `q56_emis_pricing`** (`equations.gms:15-17`):
```gams
q56_emis_pricing(i2, pollutants, emis_annual) ..
  v56_emis_pricing(i2, emis_annual, pollutants) =e=
    vm_emissions_reg(i2, emis_annual, pollutants);
```

This applies to `emis_annual` sources. Land-use-change CO2 is classified as `emis_oneoff`, not `emis_annual`, so `q52_emis_co2_actual`'s output (`vm_emissions_reg(i, emis_oneoff, "co2_c")`) feeds into Pathway B.

### Pathway B: vm_carbon_stock directly through q56_emis_pricing_co2 (primary CO2 pathway)

For one-off CO2 from land-use change, Module 56 computes pricing **directly from carbon stocks**, intentionally bypassing the `vm_emissions_reg` computed by Module 52:

**Equation `q56_emis_pricing_co2`** (`equations.gms:19-22`):
```gams
q56_emis_pricing_co2(i2, emis_oneoff) ..
  v56_emis_pricing(i2, emis_oneoff, "co2_c") =e=
    sum((cell(i2,j2), emis_land(emis_oneoff, land, c_pools)),
      (pcm_carbon_stock(j2, land, c_pools, "actual")
       - vm_carbon_stock(j2, land, c_pools, "%c56_carbon_stock_pricing%"))
      / m_timestep_length);
```

Key differences from `q52_emis_co2_actual`:
1. **Carbon stock type is configurable**: `%c56_carbon_stock_pricing%` is a switch — either `"actual"` (all carbon changes priced) or `"actualNoAcEst"` (default — excludes afforestation establishment from pricing to avoid double-counting with CDR rewards). Module 52's equation always uses `"actual"`.
2. **This is the authoritative CO2 figure for pricing**: Module 56 computes the pricing base directly from stocks, not from `vm_emissions_reg`. `q52_emis_co2_actual` and `q56_emis_pricing_co2` are structurally parallel but serve different purposes: M52 feeds `vm_emissions_reg` for reporting/GDX output; M56 reads stocks independently for economic optimization.

### Step 5: Cost aggregation

**Equation `q56_emission_cost_oneoff`** (`equations.gms:45-52`) converts the pricing-base emissions into costs with an annuity factor:
```gams
q56_emission_cost_oneoff(i2, emis_oneoff) ..
  v56_emission_cost(i2, emis_oneoff) =e=
    sum(pollutants,
      v56_emis_pricing(i2, emis_oneoff, pollutants)
      * m_timestep_length
      * sum(ct,
          im_pollutant_prices(ct, i2, pollutants, emis_oneoff)
          * pm_interest(ct, i2) / (1 + pm_interest(ct, i2))));
```

The `m_timestep_length` factor reconverts the annual rate back to a total stock change; multiplying by `r/(1+r)` (annuity due factor with infinite horizon) levels a one-time emission against annually recurring emissions in the objective.

`im_pollutant_prices(t, i, pollutants, emis_source)` (configured in `preloop.gms:35-91`) is zeroed by the emission policy matrix `f56_emis_policy` for any source not covered by the selected policy. Under the default policy `reddnatveg_nosoil`, CO2 from `primforest_vegc`, `primforest_litc`, `secdforest_vegc`, `secdforest_litc`, `other_vegc`, `other_litc`, and peatland is priced; soil carbon and cropland/pasture/forestry stocks are excluded (`preloop.gms:84-91`).

**Equation `q56_emission_costs`** (`equations.gms:56-58`) aggregates across all sources:
```gams
q56_emission_costs(i2) ..
  vm_emission_costs(i2) =e=
    sum(emis_source, v56_emission_cost(i2, emis_source));
```

`vm_emission_costs(i)` is declared in Module 56 (`declarations.gms:39`) and enters Module 11 (Costs) → objective function. This is the final link from carbon stock dynamics to economic incentives that shape all land-use decisions.

## Summary of the chain

```
LPJmL inputs → fm_carbon_density (M52 input.gms:16-20)
  → pm_carbon_density_*_ac (M52 start.gms; optionally calibrated in preloop.gms)
    → Land modules (30, 31, 32, 35) compute vm_carbon_stock during solve
      → M52 q52_emis_co2_actual (equations.gms:16-19)
            writes vm_emissions_reg(i, emis_oneoff, "co2_c") [for reporting]
      → M56 q56_emis_pricing_co2 (equations.gms:19-22)
            reads vm_carbon_stock directly → v56_emis_pricing(i, emis_oneoff, "co2_c") [for pricing]
            (config: c56_carbon_stock_pricing; policy filter: f56_emis_policy)
        → M56 q56_emission_cost_oneoff (equations.gms:45-52)
              v56_emission_cost = v56_emis_pricing × m_timestep_length × price × r/(1+r)
          → M56 q56_emission_costs (equations.gms:56-58)
                vm_emission_costs(i) → Module 11 objective function
```
