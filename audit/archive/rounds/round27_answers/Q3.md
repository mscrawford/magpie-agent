## 1. Core Variables

**Pasture area**: `vm_land(j,"past")` (mio. ha)
- Declared by Module 10 (`declarations.gms:14-24`), used by Module 31 in every equation (`equations.gms:17,23,39,43`).
- This is an ENDOGENOUS optimization variable; Module 10 determines its value subject to the land conservation constraint and the demand-driven constraints Module 31 places on production.
- Conservation lower bound: `presolve.gms:9` sets `vm_land.lo(j,"past") = sum(consv_type, pm_land_conservation(t,j,"past",consv_type))` — legally protected grassland cannot be converted away.

**Pasture production**: `vm_prod(j,"pasture")` (mio. tDM per yr)
- Declared in Module 17 (Production) but constrained in Module 31.
- Represents cellular pasture biomass available for livestock grazing.

## 2. Key Equations in Module 31 (endo_jun13)

Five equations total; the first is the load-bearing one for this trace.

### q31_prod — Production Capacity Constraint

**Location**: `equations.gms:16-18`

```gams
vm_prod(j2,"pasture") =l= vm_land(j2,"past") * vm_yld(j2,"pasture","rainfed")
```

- **Constraint type**: Inequality (=l=), not equality. Pasture may be underutilized; the model is not forced to graze every hectare to its maximum potential yield.
- **Meaning**: Pasture biomass production in any cell cannot exceed the product of pasture area and rainfed pasture yield. This is the equation that makes pasture area endogenous: because livestock feed demand (from Module 70 via 16 and 21) must be satisfied, the optimizer expands `vm_land(j,"past")` until this upper bound is not binding — or until competing land uses make expansion too costly.
- Only rainfed yields are used; there is no irrigated pasture option in this realization.

### q31_carbon — Above-Ground Carbon Stocks

**Location**: `equations.gms:22-24`

```gams
vm_carbon_stock(j2,"past",ag_pools,stockType) =e=
  m_carbon_stock(vm_land,fm_carbon_density,"past")
```

- Computes above-ground carbon (vegetation + litter pools) from pasture area and density parameters. Below-ground carbon is handled by Module 59.

### q31_cost_prod_past — Calibration Cost Artifact

**Location**: `equations.gms:31-32`

```gams
vm_cost_prod_past(i2) =e= sum(cell(i2,j2), vm_prod(j2,"pasture")) * s31_fac_req_past
```

- `s31_fac_req_past = 1 USD17MER/tDM` only during the initial calibration timestep (set in `input.gms:10`), then zeroed in `postsolve.gms:10`. This small cost prevents the optimizer from allocating excessive pasture area during calibration.
- Feeds into the Module 11 objective function via `vm_cost_prod_past(i)`.

### q31_bv_manpast / q31_bv_rangeland — Biodiversity Values

**Locations**: `equations.gms:38-40` and `equations.gms:42-44`

```gams
vm_bv(j2,"manpast",potnatveg) =e=
  vm_land(j2,"past") * fm_luh2_side_layers(j2,"manpast")
  * fm_bii_coeff("manpast",potnatveg) * fm_luh2_side_layers(j2,potnatveg)

vm_bv(j2,"rangeland",potnatveg) =e=
  vm_land(j2,"past") * fm_luh2_side_layers(j2,"rangeland")
  * fm_bii_coeff("rangeland",potnatveg) * fm_luh2_side_layers(j2,potnatveg)
```

- LUH2 side layers (`fm_luh2_side_layers`) provide fixed shares of total pasture area that is "managed pasture" vs. "rangeland". These shares are NOT endogenous — the model cannot optimize grazing intensity.
- Separate BII coefficients (`fm_bii_coeff`) apply to each type; rangeland typically retains higher biodiversity than managed pasture.
- Results fed to Module 44 (Biodiversity).

## 3. How Module 14 Provides Pasture Yields

Source: `module_14.md`.

**Default realization**: `managementcalib_aug19`.

The yield variable `vm_yld(j,"pasture","rainfed")` (tDM/ha/yr) is the direct input to `q31_prod`. Module 14 calculates it via equation:

**q14_yield_past** (`equations.gms:35-39`):

```gams
q14_yield_past(j2,w) ..
 vm_yld(j2,"pasture",w) =e=
 sum(ct,(i14_yields_calib(ct,j2,"pasture",w))
 * sum(cell(i2,j2),pm_past_mngmnt_factor(ct,i2)))
 * (1 + s14_yld_past_switch*(sum((cell(i2,j2), supreg(h2,i2)), pcm_tau(j2, "crop")/fm_tau1995(h2)) - 1));
```

Three multiplicative components:

1. **Calibrated baseline** (`i14_yields_calib`): LPJmL climate-driven potential yields, corrected to historical modeled pasture productivity via `p14_pyield_corr` (preloop.gms:14-27; freeze point pushed to y2020 as of commit `c7731e234`), then calibrated through the lambda-based limited calibration system (Heinke 2013, `preloop.gms:26-109`).

2. **Exogenous management factor** (`pm_past_mngmnt_factor(ct,i)`): Provided by Module 70 (Livestock). This reflects regional differences in grazing intensity and management practices; it is NOT optimized within GAMS — it is a pre-computed exogenous trajectory linked to livestock demand projections.

3. **Crop-sector spillover** (`s14_yld_past_switch = 0.25` by default, `input.gms:20`): 25% of the improvement in crop-sector tau (`pcm_tau`, the PREVIOUS timestep's tau at cluster level) above the 1995 baseline spills over to pasture yields. Pasture uses the previous-timestep tau rather than the current one, reflecting a lag in knowledge transfer from crop to pasture systems.

**Key limitation** (stated in `realization.gms:23-24`): Pasture intensification is exogenous; the model cannot endogenously respond to land scarcity by intensifying pasture management. `s14_degradation = 0` by default, so degradation effects on yields are off.

## 4. How Module 70 Drives Pasture Demand

Source: `module_70.md`.

**Default realization**: `fbask_jan16`.

Module 70 converts livestock production targets (`vm_prod_reg(i,kap)`) into feed demand across feed types including "pasture" via **q70_feed** (`equations.gms:17-20`):

```gams
vm_dem_feed(i2,kap,kall) =g=
    vm_prod_reg(i2,kap) * sum(ct, im_feed_baskets(ct,i2,kap,kall))
    + sum(ct, vm_feed_balanceflow(i2,kap,kall))
```

- `im_feed_baskets(ct,i,kap,"pasture")` gives tDM pasture required per tDM livestock product — pre-calculated from bio-energetic regression models outside GAMS.
- `vm_feed_balanceflow(i,kli_rum,"pasture")`: For ruminants, balance flows reconcile model estimates with FAO feed statistics and handle scavenging/roadside grazing. In historical periods this is exogenous (`fm_feed_balanceflow`); once the scavenging flag triggers (`p70_endo_scavenging_flag > 0` in `presolve.gms:14-20`), future pasture demand is endogenously reduced by 38.5% of baseline to represent scavenging (`s70_scavenging_ratio = 0.385`, `input.gms:27`).

**Transmission path** from feed demand to pasture area:

```
Module 70: vm_dem_feed(i,kap,"pasture")
    → Module 16 (Demand): aggregated with food/material/bioenergy demands
    → Module 21 (Trade): regional supply = regional demand balance
    → Module 31: vm_prod(j,"pasture") must satisfy regional demand
    → q31_prod: vm_prod ≤ vm_land * vm_yld  [binding upper bound]
    → Module 10: vm_land(j,"past") allocated by optimization
```

The inequality in `q31_prod` means the optimizer sets `vm_land(j,"past")` just large enough that sufficient pasture biomass can be produced to meet the aggregated ruminant feed demand, trading off against land conversion costs from other modules.

## 5. How Pasture Area Enters the Land Balance (Module 10)

Source: `module_10.md`.

**Default realization**: `landmatrix_dec18` (only realization).

Module 10 is the central land allocation hub. The key equation is:

**q10_land_area** (`equations.gms:13-15`):

```gams
q10_land_area(j2) ..
  sum(land, vm_land(j2,land)) =e=
  sum(land, pcm_land(j2,land));
```

- Total land area in each cell j is conserved exactly between timesteps.
- `vm_land(j,"past")` is one of 7 land types (`crop`, `past`, `forestry`, `primforest`, `secdforest`, `urban`, `other`) that must sum to the fixed cell total.
- `pcm_land(j,land)` is updated in `postsolve.gms:9` after each solve to carry forward the previous timestep's allocation for the recursive dynamics.

Pasture expansion and contraction are tracked through the transition matrix:

- **q10_transition_to** (`equations.gms:19-21`): `sum(land_from, vm_lu_transitions(j,land_from,"past")) =e= vm_land(j,"past")` — all land flowing into pasture must account for the new pasture area.
- **q10_transition_from** (`equations.gms:23-25`): `sum(land_to, vm_lu_transitions(j,"past",land_to)) =e= pcm_land(j,"past")` — all land flowing out of previous pasture must be accounted for.
- **q10_landexpansion/q10_landreduction** (`equations.gms:30-38`): derived from off-diagonal transition matrix elements; small costs (`q10_cost`, `equations.gms:42-44`, 1 USD/ha) discourage unrealistic within-timestep cycling.

**Conservation interaction**: Module 22 provides `pm_land_conservation(t,j,"past",consv_type)`, which Module 31 uses in `presolve.gms:9` to set the lower bound `vm_land.lo(j,"past")`. This floor propagates through the land balance: if protected pasture area is positive, the optimizer cannot convert that pasture to other uses even if livestock demand falls.

## 6. Full Causal Chain Summary

```
Module 70 (Livestock)
  im_feed_baskets × vm_prod_reg → vm_dem_feed(i,kap,"pasture")      [q70_feed]

Module 16 (Demand) aggregates vm_dem_feed into commodity totals

Module 21 (Trade) imposes regional supply ≥ demand constraint for "pasture"

Module 17 (Production) sets vm_prod(j,"pasture") to satisfy regional supply

Module 31 (Pasture, endo_jun13)
  vm_prod(j,"pasture") ≤ vm_land(j,"past") × vm_yld(j,"pasture","rainfed")    [q31_prod]
      ↑                            ↑
  must satisfy              from Module 10
  livestock demand          land balance
                                  ↑
                         subject to land conservation:
                         sum(land, vm_land(j,land)) = sum(land, pcm_land(j,land))   [q10_land_area]

Module 14 (Yields, managementcalib_aug19)
  vm_yld(j,"pasture","rainfed") = i14_yields_calib × pm_past_mngmnt_factor
                                 × [1 + 0.25 × (pcm_tau/fm_tau1995 - 1)]             [q14_yield_past]
```

**Net effect**: Higher livestock production targets (from population/income growth via Module 09/15) increase `vm_dem_feed(i,"pasture")`, which tightens the `q31_prod` constraint, which forces the optimizer to expand `vm_land(j,"past")`. This expansion can only come at the cost of some other land type (forest, other natural vegetation, cropland) because `q10_land_area` enforces total cell area conservation. The magnitude of pasture expansion depends on `vm_yld(j,"pasture","rainfed")` from Module 14 — higher yields allow meeting the same demand with less area.
