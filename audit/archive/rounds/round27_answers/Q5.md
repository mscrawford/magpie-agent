## Module 20 (Processing) — Default Realization: `substitution_may21`

### Default Realization

The default realization is **`substitution_may21`** (supersedes `substitution_dec18`, which it differs from by removing calibration factors that artificially constrained oil substitution; `realization.gms:9`).

### Processing Routes

Seven physical process types are defined in `sets.gms:20-24`:
milling (cereals), refining (sugar crops), extracting (oilcrops), distilling (cereals/sugarcane), fermentation, breeding (SCP), ginning (cotton), plus a virtual "substitutes" activity.

Processable products (set **kpr**) include cereals, oilcrops, sugar crops, potato, cassava, cotton, bioenergy grasses, and selected secondary products (`sets.gms:10-13`). Non-processable products (set **knpr**) — animal products, residues, alcohol, etc. — have processing demand fixed to zero at `presolve.gms:9` via `vm_dem_processing.fx(i,knpr) = 0`.

### The Central Conversion Equation: `q20_processing`

**Location**: `equations.gms:59-65`

**Formula**:
```
sum(processing20, v20_dem_processing(i2,processing20,kpr)
    * sum(ct, i20_processing_conversion_factors(ct,processing20,ksd,kpr)))
  =e=
  (vm_prod_reg(i2,ksd) - sum(ct, f20_processing_balanceflow(ct,i2,ksd)))
    * sum(ct, i20_processing_shares(ct,i2,ksd,kpr))
  - v20_secondary_substitutes(i2,ksd,kpr)
  + vm_secondary_overproduction(i2,ksd,kpr)
```

**Left side**: The quantity of primary product `kpr` demanded via all process routes, weighted by **globally uniform** conversion factors `i20_processing_conversion_factors` — how many tonnes of secondary product `ksd` one tonne of `kpr` yields.

**Right side — four components**:

1. `vm_prod_reg(i2,ksd)` — regional production of secondary product `ksd`, supplied by **module 17** (Production). This is the primary link to the supply side.
2. `f20_processing_balanceflow(ct,i2,ksd)` — a static regional offset in `f20_processing_balanceflow.cs3` (`input.gms:10-13`) capturing historical country-specific deviations from global average conversion efficiency. Subtracted from regional production to keep conversion factors globally uniform while accommodating real-world data.
3. `i20_processing_shares(ct,i2,ksd,kpr)` — **region-specific** shares indicating what fraction of secondary product `ksd` in region `i` is sourced from primary product `kpr` (e.g., more palm oil in SE Asia, more rapeseed oil in EU). Source: `f20_processing_shares.cs3` (`input.gms:20-23`).
4. `v20_secondary_substitutes` and `vm_secondary_overproduction` — substitution and overproduction adjustments (see below).

**Why global conversion factors + regional shares?** Conversion factors are global to avoid locking future projections into historical regional processing patterns (`equations.gms:46-48`); regional heterogeneity in the crop mix used to produce a given secondary product is captured by the shares (`equations.gms:51-52`).

### Three Aggregation Equations

These link the internal per-route variable `v20_dem_processing` to interface variables and handle FAO Commodity Balance Sheet accounting conventions:

**`q20_processing_aggregation_nocereals`** (`equations.gms:22-24`)
```
vm_dem_processing(i2,kpr) =e=
  sum(no_milling_ginning20, v20_dem_processing(i2,no_milling_ginning20,kpr))
```
Aggregates processing demand across all routes except milling and ginning (set `no_milling_ginning20` = {refining, extracting, distilling, fermentation, breeding, substitutes}; `sets.gms:29-30`). This is the aggregate consumed by module 16.

**`q20_processing_aggregation_cereals`** (`equations.gms:32-33`)
```
vm_dem_food(i2,kcereals20) =e= v20_dem_processing(i2,"milling",kcereals20)
```
Directly equates cereal milling to food demand (`vm_dem_food`, the module 16 interface variable) because FAO Commodity Balance Sheets do not classify cereal milling as "processing" — it is attributed to food use. Cereals in set `kcereals20` = {tece, maiz, trce, rice_pro} (`sets.gms:26-27`).

**`q20_processing_aggregation_cotton`** (`equations.gms:40-41`)
```
vm_prod_reg(i2,"cottn_pro") =e= v20_dem_processing(i2,"ginning","cottn_pro")
```
Equates cotton ginning to cotton production (module 17 variable) because FAO does not separately track seed cotton vs. lint/seed — production of cotton lint is implicitly bound to raw cotton production.

### Substitution Equations (4)

When coupled byproducts are in short supply, substitutes can fill the gap, at a strong penalty. Four equations gate this:

| Equation | Location | Purpose |
|----------|---------|---------|
| `q20_processing_substitution_oils` | `equations.gms:68-70` | Any oilcrop can substitute for "oils" (mass-based) |
| `q20_processing_substitution_sugar` | `equations.gms:73-75` | Sugar can substitute for molasses |
| `q20_processing_substitution_protein` | `equations.gms:81-89` | Oilcakes/distillers grain substituted by oilcrop/oilcake on **nitrogen-equivalent** basis (using `fm_attributes("nr",...)`) |
| `q20_processing_substitution_brans` | `equations.gms:93-97` | Cereals substitute for brans on nitrogen-equivalent basis |

Bounds enabling substitution are set in `presolve.gms:14-19`; all substitution is fixed to zero by default and opened to Inf selectively for the four product classes above. The penalty in `q20_substitution_utility_loss` (`equations.gms:134-142`) charges a flat **200 USD/tDM** for any substitution demand plus a quality-differential adjustment for oils (`f20_quality_cost`), strongly discouraging substitution unless price differences are very large.

---

### Cost Equations

**`q20_processing_costs`** (`equations.gms:115-121`): Calculates `vm_cost_processing(i)` as the sum over all (ksd, processing20, kpr) of (demand × conversion factor × unit cost `i20_processing_unitcosts`), plus a separate SCP term using `f20_scp_unitcosts` because hydrogen-based SCP has no feedstock and its unit processing costs are zeroed to avoid double-counting (`preloop.gms:13-16`).

**`q20_substitution_utility_loss`** (`equations.gms:134-142`): Calculates `vm_processing_substitution_cost(i)` — a utility-loss cost fed to module 11.

Both cost variables flow directly into module 11 (Costs) and thus the objective function.

---

### Relationships to Other Modules

**Module 17 (Production) → Module 20**
`vm_prod_reg(i2,ksd)` appears on the right-hand side of `q20_processing` as the regional production of secondary products. Module 20 does NOT determine how much of a secondary product is produced; it links primary processing demand to whatever module 17's optimization yields. If more secondary product is produced than needed, `vm_secondary_overproduction` absorbs the surplus.

**Module 20 → Module 16 (Demand)**
`vm_dem_processing` (aggregated over non-milling/ginning routes) is one of the demand categories that module 16 must satisfy — it represents the quantity of primary products demanded for processing into secondary products. `vm_secondary_overproduction` is routed to the waste category in module 16's demand accounting. The cereal milling link runs the reverse direction: module 20 reads `vm_dem_food` from module 16 and equates cereal milling to it (`q20_processing_aggregation_cereals`).

**Module 21 (Trade)**
Module 20 does NOT directly interface with module 21. Trade operates at the regional level on `vm_prod_reg` and demand aggregates; module 20 receives the post-trade regional production of secondary products through `vm_prod_reg`. The processing module sees net regional availability of secondaries, not the trade flows themselves. (No vm_trade_* variables appear in module_20.md's declared interface inputs or outputs.)

**Module 11 (Costs)**
`vm_cost_processing` and `vm_processing_substitution_cost` enter the cost aggregation in module 11 and thereby the optimization objective.

### Summary

Module 20's default realization `substitution_may21` converts primary agricultural products to secondary products via `q20_processing` (`equations.gms:59-65`), which balances (primary input × global conversion factor) against (regional secondary production from module 17 × regional processing share − substitutes + overproduction). Three aggregation equations link per-route internal variables to the interface: two special-case FAO conventions (cereals→food, cotton→production) and one general aggregation for all other routes into `vm_dem_processing`, which module 16 uses as a demand category. Module 21 is not a direct interface partner; trade affects module 20 only indirectly through the `vm_prod_reg` values it shapes. Costs flow to module 11 via two dedicated cost variables.
