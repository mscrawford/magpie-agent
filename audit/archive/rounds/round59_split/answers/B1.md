## 1. Land types Module 35 manages

Three top-level natural-vegetation pools (`land_natveg = {primforest, secdforest, other}`, per `modules/module_10.md:166`), all slices of the shared `vm_land(j,land)` variable declared in Module 10:

| Pool | Variable | Age-class tracked? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary forest | `vm_land(j,"primforest")` | No (assumed mature) | One-way: can only decrease (`modules/module_35.md:89`, `:742-746`) |
| Secondary forest | `v35_secdforest(j,ac)` → aggregated to `vm_land(j,"secdforest")` | Yes, `ac0…ac300,acx` (62 classes, 5-yr intervals) | `modules/module_35.md:94-100,134` |
| Other natural land | `vm_land_other(j,othertype35,ac)` → aggregated to `vm_land(j,"other")` | Yes, same age-class set | Two subtypes: `"othernat"` (natural grassland/savanna/shrubland) and `"youngsecdf"` (young secondary forest, <20 tC/ha) — `modules/module_35.md:102-106` |

The forest/other split is governed by a **hard 20 tC/ha vegetation-carbon threshold**, not a continuous transition (`modules/module_35.md:109-125`, code at `presolve.gms:109-122`).

## 2. Equations governing the pools and their transitions

All citations are to `modules/35_natveg/pot_forest_may24/equations.gms` unless noted; 32 equations total (`modules/module_35.md:362`).

### 2.1 Pool aggregation (defines `vm_land` for secdforest/other)

- **`q35_land_secdforest`** (`equations.gms:11`):
  `vm_land(j2,"secdforest") =e= sum(ac, v35_secdforest(j2,ac))`
- **`q35_land_other`** (`equations.gms:13`):
  `vm_land(j2,"other") =e= sum((othertype35,ac), vm_land_other(j2,othertype35,ac))`

Primary forest has **no analogous aggregation equation** — `vm_land(j,"primforest")` is a bare optimization variable (age-classes not tracked), bounded directly rather than built up from sub-components (see §4).

### 2.2 Transitions between age classes / pools

- **`q35_secdforest_regeneration`** (`equations.gms:208-214`): new secdforest in establishment age classes = harvested secdforest + harvested **primforest** + restoration:
  `sum(ac_est, v35_secdforest(j2,ac_est)) =e= sum(ac_sub,v35_hvarea_secdforest(j2,ac_sub)) + v35_hvarea_primforest(j2) + p35_land_restoration(j2,"secdforest")`
  This is the formal mechanism by which **logged/harvested** primary forest becomes secondary forest — a documented one-way transition (`modules/module_35.md:567,78-79`). **Scope caveat (important — see §6)**: `v35_hvarea_primforest` is only the *harvested subset* of total primforest reduction (`v35_hvarea_primforest(j2) =l= v35_primforest_reduction(j2)`, `q35_hvarea_primforest`, `equations.gms:181-184`) — i.e. this equation governs what happens to primforest area that is **logged**, not primforest area that is **directly deforested/converted** to cropland, pasture, or urban. Those conversions are a separate, fully-permitted pathway via Module 10's `vm_lu_transitions` that does not route through secdforest at all (§6).
- **`q35_other_regeneration`** (`equations.gms:218-223`): new `othernat` establishment area = harvested other land (both subtypes) + land expansion into "other" from Module 10 (`vm_landexpansion(j2,"other")`).
- **`q35_secdforest_est`** / **`q35_other_est`** (`equations.gms:228-229`, `:231-232`): distribute new area equally across `ac_est` establishment classes.
- **`q35_max_forest_establishment`** (`equations.gms:196-201`): total forest expansion (natural + managed, `land_forest`) ≤ `pm_max_forest_est(t,j)` minus existing `youngsecdf` — this is the constraint Module 35 hands to Module 32 (forestry) for plantation siting.
- **Age-class aging** (not an equation — a `presolve.gms` parameter shift, `presolve.gms:84-97`, `modules/module_35.md:138-155`): `s35_shift = m_timestep_length_forestry/5`; each age class shifts forward by `s35_shift` positions per timestep, with the tail accumulating into `acx` (the absorbing mature class).
- **Maturation (youngsecdf → secdforest)** (`presolve.gms:109-122`, `modules/module_35.md:111-128`): area with `pm_carbon_density_secdforest_ac_uncalib(t,j,ac,"vegc") > 20` tC/ha is reclassified from `youngsecdf` (other land) to secdforest — a presolve parameter reassignment, not a GAMS equation.

### 2.3 Land-change bookkeeping (expansion/reduction/diff — feeds Module 10)

- **`q35_other_expansion`** (`equations.gms:100-102`), **`q35_other_reduction`** (`:104-106`), **`q35_secdforest_expansion`** (`:108-110`), **`q35_secdforest_reduction`** (`:112-114`), **`q35_primforest_reduction`** (`:116-118`): define gross expansion/reduction per pool as area-in-establishment-classes vs. previous-minus-current area.
- **`q35_natforest_reduction`** (`equations.gms:84-85`): `vm_natforest_reduction(j2) =e= v35_primforest_reduction(j2) + sum(ac_sub, v35_secdforest_reduction(j2,ac_sub))` — consumed by Module 32 (`q32_ndc_aff_limit`).
- **`q35_landdiff`** (`equations.gms:92-98`): sums all expansion/reduction terms into the scalar `vm_landdiff_natveg` — this is the interface variable Module 10 consumes (see §6).

### 2.4 Conservation constraints (see §5 for the full Module 22 chain)

- **`q35_natveg_conservation`** (`equations.gms:19-22`): `sum(land_natveg, vm_land(j2,land_natveg)) =g= sum((ct,land_natveg), pm_land_conservation(ct,j2,land_natveg,"protect"))`.
- **`q35_min_forest`** (`equations.gms:78-80`) / **`q35_min_other`** (`equations.gms:82`): NPI/NDC-specific floors, `=g= p35_min_forest`/`p35_min_other` — a **separate** policy layer from Module 22 (see §5).
- **`q35_secdforest_restoration`** (`equations.gms:24-28`) / **`q35_other_restoration`** (`equations.gms:30-33`): transitions into secdforest/other from `vm_lu_transitions` must meet the restoration targets Module 35 itself computes from Module 22's data (`p35_land_restoration`).

### 2.5 Carbon, BII, harvest, production equations (for completeness)

- **`q35_carbon_primforest`** (`equations.gms:42-44`): `vm_carbon_stock(...) =e= m_carbon_stock(vm_land,fm_carbon_density,"primforest")`.
- **`q35_carbon_secdforest`** (`equations.gms:49-51`): area-weighted over age classes using the **blended** density `p35_carbon_density_secdforest` (FRA-calibrated + uncalibrated curves weighted by natural-origin share; blend formula at `presolve.gms:248-252`, see `modules/module_35.md:344-354`).
- **`q35_carbon_other`** (`equations.gms:53-55`).
- **`q35_bv_primforest`/`q35_bv_secdforest`/`q35_bv_other`** (`equations.gms:59-61,63-66,68-71`): biodiversity value = area × age-class BII coefficient × LUH2 potential-vegetation layer.
- **`q35_hvarea_secdforest`/`_primforest`/`_other`** (`:176-179,181-184,186-189`): harvested area ≤ area reduction.
- **`q35_prod_secdforest`/`_primforest`/`_other`** (`:144-147,153-156,162-168`): timber production = harvested area × growing stock / timestep length.
- **`q35_cost_hvarea`** (`equations.gms:132-138`): harvest costs, differentiated by pool ($3,690/ha primforest > $3,075/ha other > $2,460/ha secdforest; `modules/module_35.md:700-702`).

## 3. Age-class dynamics and disturbance (mechanism, not equations)

All of §3 is `presolve.gms` parameter arithmetic on `pc35_secdforest`/`pc35_land_other`/`pcm_land`, executed **before** the solve, not GAMS equations:

1. **Aging shift** (`presolve.gms:84-97`): age classes advance by `s35_shift = m_timestep_length_forestry/5` positions; overflow accumulates in `acx`.
2. **Disturbance** (`presolve.gms:12-45`, `s35_forest_damage` switch, 0–4): removes area proportionally from existing age classes and re-adds it to the youngest establishment classes (`ac_est`). **Default mode = 2** ("shifting agriculture fade-out", `modules/module_35.md:182-198`): fixed regional loss rates from `f35_forest_lost_share`, multiplied by `(1 - p35_damage_fader(t))`, fading to zero by `s35_forest_damage_end = 2050`. These are historical/fixed data-driven rates, **not** a mechanistic fire or disturbance simulation (`modules/module_35_notes.md:11`).
3. **Recovery from abandonment** (`presolve.gms:48-78`): land freed by forestry or agricultural abandonment is split between `youngsecdf` (recovering toward forest, capped by `pm_max_forest_est`) and `othernat` (residual), per `modules/module_35.md:261-297`.
4. **Maturation** (`presolve.gms:109-122`, 20 tC/ha threshold, uncalibrated curve as of 2026-04-20 per `modules/module_35.md:125`).
5. **Natural-origin tracking** (`presolve.gms` various, added 2026-04-20, `modules/module_35.md:301-356`): a parallel bookkeeping parameter `p35_secdforest_natural(t,j,ac)` tracks the fraction of secdforest area that arose from natural succession (vs. existing/managed legacy forest), used to blend the carbon-density curve in `q35_carbon_secdforest` and to raise `v35_secdforest.lo` so natural-origin area cannot be harvested.

## 4. How `vm_land` is populated for the natveg slices

This differs materially by pool:

- **`vm_land(j,"secdforest")`**: fully determined by `q35_land_secdforest` as the sum of `v35_secdforest(j,ac)` over all age classes — i.e., it is a *derived* aggregate of a lower-level, age-resolved optimization variable.
- **`vm_land(j,"other")`**: likewise derived via `q35_land_other`, aggregating `vm_land_other(j,othertype35,ac)` over both subtypes and all age classes.
- **`vm_land(j,"primforest")`**: **not** built from an aggregation equation. It is a standalone (non-age-resolved) decision variable whose value is constrained by:
  - a **lower bound** set in presolve (`presolve.gms:155-160`, `modules/module_35.md:738-746`): during the historical period, `vm_land.lo(j,"primforest") = 0`; thereafter, `vm_land.lo(j,"primforest") = (1 - s35_natveg_harvest_shr) * pcm_land(j,"primforest")` (default `s35_natveg_harvest_shr = 1`, i.e., up to 100% harvestable subject to other constraints);
  - the **disturbance loss** applied directly to `pcm_land(j,"primforest")` in presolve (`presolve.gms:35-39,244`, `modules/module_35.md:238-245`) *before* the solve, which shifts the effective starting stock down;
  - Module 10's transition-matrix balance equations (`q10_transition_to`/`q10_transition_from`), which require `vm_land(j,"primforest")` to reconcile with whatever `vm_lu_transitions(j,·,"primforest")`/`vm_lu_transitions(j,"primforest",·)` flows are permitted — and Module 10 fixes essentially all inflows to zero (no land can *become* primary forest; `cross_module/land_balance_conservation.md:194-199`), so `vm_land(j,"primforest")` can only fall over time, consistent with the doc's "one-way decrease" characterization. **On outflows**: per the transition-matrix table in `cross_module/land_balance_conservation.md:140-150` and the restriction list at `:188-207`, only `primforest→forestry` and `primforest→other` are explicitly forced to zero; `primforest→crop`, `primforest→past`, and `primforest→urban` are all marked ✓ (allowed) in that table and are **not** in the forbidden list — i.e. Module 10 permits primary forest to be directly deforested/converted straight to cropland, pasture, or urban via `vm_lu_transitions`, with no requirement that it pass through secdforest first. The primforest→secdforest pathway described in §2.2 (`q35_secdforest_regeneration`) is a *separate, additional* channel specific to the harvested/logged portion of primforest loss — it is not the only, or necessarily the dominant, fate of shrinking primforest area. Do not read "harvested primforest becomes secdforest" as "all primforest loss becomes secdforest."
  - `q35_primforest_reduction` (`equations.gms:116-118`) then just *records* the resulting reduction (`pcm_land(j2,"primforest") - vm_land(j2,"primforest")`) for use in `q35_natforest_reduction`/`q35_landdiff`; it does not itself set `vm_land`.

So in summary: secdforest and other-land values are **bottom-up aggregates** of age-class variables via explicit `=e=` equations; primforest is a **top-down bounded variable** whose value emerges from presolve-set bounds, presolve-applied disturbance losses, and Module 10's transition restrictions, with no age structure.

## 5. How Module 22's conservation constraint restricts Module 35

Module 22 (`area_based_apr22`, `modules/module_22.md:6`) is a pure data-provider (no equations of its own) that computes `pm_land_conservation(t,j,land,consv_type)` for `consv_type ∈ {protect, restore}` in `presolve_ini` (`modules/module_22.md:63,152-153`). For natural vegetation it restricts Module 35 through **two distinct, layered mechanisms**:

1. **Aggregate floor** — `q35_natveg_conservation` (`equations.gms:19-22`): total natural land (primforest + secdforest + other, summed) must be ≥ the sum of Module-22 protection targets across those three land types. This is an aggregate `=g=` — it does **not** by itself pin any individual pool.
2. **Per-age-class lower bound on secdforest** — set in presolve (`presolve.gms:172-180`, quoted verbatim in `modules/module_35.md:813-825`):
   ```gams
   p35_protection_dist(j,ac_sub)$(...) = pc35_secdforest(j,ac_sub) / sum(ac_sub2,pc35_secdforest(j,ac_sub2));
   ...
   v35_secdforest.lo(j,ac_sub) = max(
       pm_land_conservation(t,j,"secdforest","protect") * p35_protection_dist(j,ac_sub),
       pc35_secdforest_natural(j,ac_sub)   [historical period]
   );
   * non-historical period additionally includes the harvest-share floor:
   v35_secdforest.lo(j,ac_sub) = max(
       (1-s35_natveg_harvest_shr) * pc35_secdforest(j,ac_sub),
       pm_land_conservation(t,j,"secdforest","protect") * p35_protection_dist(j,ac_sub),
       pc35_secdforest_natural(j,ac_sub)
   );
   ```
   This distributes Module 22's aggregate `"protect"` target proportionally across age classes (weighted by current-period area share), and additionally protects natural-origin secdforest area unconditionally.

The module doc's general presolve range (`presolve.gms:140-234`) is described as also "setting lower bounds on land areas... distributing protection across age classes... ensuring restoration targets are met" for the natveg pools generally (`modules/module_35.md:777-780`), but the only *explicit* formula quoted in the documentation I could verify this session is the secdforest one above — I did not find an explicitly quoted primforest-specific `pm_land_conservation`-driven bound distinct from the harvest-share bound in §4, so I flag that as a documentation gap rather than asserting a formula I haven't seen.

**Restoration** enters via `p35_land_restoration(j2,·)`, computed by Module 35 in presolve from Module 22's `pm_land_conservation(...,"restore")` output, and enforced through `q35_secdforest_restoration`/`q35_other_restoration` (§2.2) — transitions from agricultural land (and, for secdforest, also from forestry) into these pools must meet the restoration target.

**Important distinction — do not conflate with NPI/NDC**: `p35_min_forest`/`p35_min_other` (enforced via `q35_min_forest`/`q35_min_other`) are a **separate** policy layer, sourced from Module 35's own NPI/NDC input data `f35_min_land_stock(t,j,"%c35_ad_policy%",·)` (default `c35_ad_policy = "npi"`, `modules/module_35.md:786-796`), **not** from Module 22's `pm_land_conservation`. Both constraints can bind simultaneously but come from independent data sources and independent equations.

Module 22 itself has no equations and never appears in Module 10's constraint set directly — `modules/module_22.md:517-521` states explicitly: *"Module 10 (Land) does NOT directly read `pm_land_conservation`. It is constrained transitively: M29, M31, M32, and M35 translate the parameter into `vm_land.lo` bounds and `=g=` constraints that Module 10's `vm_land` must satisfy."*

## 6. How this enters Module 10's land balance

Module 10 (`landmatrix_dec18`, sole realization, `modules/module_10.md:25`) enforces land balance through:

- **`q10_land_area`** (`equations.gms:13-15`): `sum(land, vm_land(j2,land)) =e= sum(land, pcm_land(j2,land))` — total land per cell is conserved. Since `vm_land(j,"primforest")`, `vm_land(j,"secdforest")`, `vm_land(j,"other")` are three of the seven summands on both sides, Module 35's pool values are directly constrained by this identity: whatever natveg area Module 35 does *not* claim is by construction claimed by crop/past/forestry/urban, and vice versa (`modules/module_35.md:925`: Module 35 is characterized in the docs as the "**RESIDUAL ALLOCATOR**" — it receives land not claimed by managed uses).
- **`q10_transition_to`/`q10_transition_from`** (`equations.gms:19-21,23-25`): the 7×7 `vm_lu_transitions(j,land_from,land_to)` matrix must balance against `vm_land` (destinations) and `pcm_land` (sources) for every land type including the three natveg pools. Restrictions relevant to natveg (`presolve.gms:10-23` of Module 10, quoted in `cross_module/land_balance_conservation.md:184-207`):
  - `vm_lu_transitions.fx(j,land_from,"primforest") = 0` for all sources except primforest itself — no land can *become* primary forest.
  - `vm_lu_transitions.fx(j,"primforest","other") = 0` and `vm_lu_transitions.fx(j,"secdforest","other") = 0` — direct primforest/secdforest → other conversions are blocked at the Module 10 transition-matrix level.
  - `vm_lu_transitions.fx(j,"primforest","forestry") = 0` — no plantations on primary forest.
  - Per `cross_module/land_balance_conservation.md:207`, the **primforest → secdforest** (harvest) and **other → secdforest** (maturation/recovery) transitions are instead "handled internally by Module 35... not through Module 10's transition matrix" in the sense that their *magnitude* is driven by Module 35's own equations (`q35_secdforest_regeneration`, the maturation presolve step) rather than by an independently-optimized Module-10 transition-restriction decision — though the resulting area movement must still be consistent with, and is recorded in, `vm_lu_transitions` so that `q10_transition_to`/`q10_transition_from` balance. I present this as documented rather than fully reconciled: the doc's transition-matrix legend (`cross_module/land_balance_conservation.md:155`) separately notes "M35 = Transition driven by Module 35 age-class dynamics (area change captured in vm_lu_transitions)", which is consistent with — the driver is M35, the accounting still flows through the matrix.
  - **This M35-driven primforest→secdforest channel covers only the harvested/logged subset of primforest loss (§2.2's scope caveat)**. The *general* fate of primforest area lost from `vm_land(j,"primforest")` is broader: the transition-matrix table (`cross_module/land_balance_conservation.md:140-150`) marks `primforest→crop`, `primforest→past`, and `primforest→urban` as ✓ (allowed, ordinary decision variables in `vm_lu_transitions`, not M35-driven) — only `primforest→forestry` and `primforest→other` are fixed to zero (`:188-207`). So deforestation of primary forest for agriculture or urban expansion is a direct, first-class transition in Module 10's matrix that does not pass through Module 35's secdforest-regeneration accounting at all. A reader should not conclude from `q35_secdforest_regeneration` that all — or even most — primforest loss becomes secondary forest.
- **`q10_landdiff`** (`equations.gms:50-54`): `vm_landdiff =e= sum((j2,land), vm_landexpansion + vm_landreduction) + vm_landdiff_natveg + vm_landdiff_forestry`. This is the explicit interface point for Module 35's **within-pool** gross changes (age-class shifts, disturbance losses, maturation — none of which show up as *between-type* `vm_lu_transitions`): Module 35's `q35_landdiff` (§2.3) computes `vm_landdiff_natveg` as the sum of all its own expansion/reduction terms, and Module 10 folds that scalar into the global gross-land-use-change objective-function penalty alongside the between-type transitions and Module 32's analogous `vm_landdiff_forestry`.

Net picture: Module 35's three pools are ordinary summands in Module 10's `q10_land_area` accounting identity and ordinary `land_to`/`land_from` members of the transition matrix (with several transitions fixed to zero to protect primary forest); in addition, Module 35 reports its internal gross churn separately via `vm_landdiff_natveg`, which Module 10 aggregates into the model-wide `vm_landdiff` metric used to penalize land-use-pattern oscillation in the objective function.
