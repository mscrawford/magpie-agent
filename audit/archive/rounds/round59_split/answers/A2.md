## Overview

Out of the box, total cropland in `vm_land(j,"crop")` is pinned down by a stack of three constraints from Module 29, plus the outer land-conservation constraint from Module 10 that all land types (including cropland) must jointly satisfy:

1. **(a)** `q29_cropland` — defines what "cropland" *is* (composition identity)
2. **(b)** `q29_avl_cropland` — caps cropland at the biophysically/suitability-available area
3. **(c)** `q29_fallow_max` — caps how much of that cropland may sit fallow (default: none)

At stock defaults, module_29.md's own R58 correction (Overview section) states that (c) is unguarded and binds `vm_fallow` to exactly zero, so composition (a) collapses to croparea alone — see the "capability vs. default" discussion below.

## (a) Total cropland composition — `q29_cropland`

**`detail_apr24`** (default realization) form (`modules/29_cropland/detail_apr24/equations.gms:11-12`, per `modules/module_29.md:129-134`):

```gams
q29_cropland(j2)..
  vm_land(j2,"crop") =e=
    sum((kcr,w), vm_area(j2,kcr,w)) + vm_fallow(j2) + sum(ac, v29_treecover(j2,ac));
```

**Meaning**: total cropland = harvested croparea (from Module 30, summed over crop types `kcr` and water supply types `w`) + fallow land (`vm_fallow`) + tree cover on cropland summed over all age classes (`sum(ac, v29_treecover(j2,ac))`).

For contrast, the non-default `simple_apr24` realization (`modules/29_cropland/simple_apr24/equations.gms:12-13`) reduces this to `vm_land(j2,"crop") =e= sum((kcr,w), vm_area(j2,kcr,w))` — croparea only, because that realization fixes `vm_fallow.fx(j)=0` and `vm_treecover.fx(j)=0` in its `preloop.gms`.

**Default-behavior caveat (documented, R58 correction in `modules/module_29.md` Overview, lines 41-66)**: under `config/default.cfg`, `s29_fallow_max = 0` and `s29_treecover_map = 0` combined with `s29_treecover_target = 0` drive both `vm_fallow` and `v29_treecover` to zero (see part (c) below and the tree-cover mechanism), so **at default settings `detail_apr24`'s `q29_cropland` is numerically identical to `simple_apr24`'s** — total cropland collapses to croparea alone. The doc is explicit that this is "an accounting identity for croparea" out of the box, not a bug.

**Interface**: `vm_land(j,"crop")` feeds directly into Module 10's land balance (see final section).

## (b) Available-cropland limit — `q29_avl_cropland`

(`modules/29_cropland/detail_apr24/equations.gms:22-23`, per `modules/module_29.md:169-173`):

```gams
q29_avl_cropland(j2)..
  vm_land(j2,"crop") =l= sum(ct, p29_avl_cropland(ct,j2));
```

**Meaning**: total cropland ≤ available cropland, where availability is a precomputed parameter, not a direct SNV subtraction inside this equation.

**How `p29_avl_cropland` is built** (`presolve.gms:35`, per `modules/module_29.md:181-185`):

```gams
p29_avl_cropland(t,j) = f29_avl_cropland(j,"%c29_marginal_land%") * (1 - p29_snv_shr(t,j));
```

- `f29_avl_cropland(j,marginal_land29)` — base suitability-constrained area, from the Zabel et al. 2014 Suitability Index map (input file `avl_cropland.cs3`), selected via the `c29_marginal_land` switch (default `q33_marginal`, i.e. Module 33's marginal-land definition).
- `p29_snv_shr(t,j)` — the semi-natural-vegetation share, folded in during presolve so the SNV reservation reduces the ceiling on cropland *before* optimization, in addition to the separate hard `q29_land_snv` constraint that reserves actual SNV land area.

At default `s29_snv_shr = s29_snv_shr_noselect = 0` (`input.gms:12-13`), `p29_snv_shr = 0`, so `p29_avl_cropland` reduces to the raw suitability map with no SNV discount.

## (c) Fallow-land maximum — `q29_fallow_max`

(`modules/29_cropland/detail_apr24/equations.gms:70-72`, per `modules/module_29.md:373-407`):

```gams
q29_fallow_max(j2)..
  vm_fallow(j2) =l= vm_land(j2,"crop") * s29_fallow_max;
```

**Meaning**: fallow land ≤ (maximum fallow share) × total cropland.

**Default**: `s29_fallow_max = 0` (`input.gms:33`). This equation is **unconditional / unguarded** — active in every cell, every timestep, with no `$()` gate. Combined with `vm_fallow` being a positive variable with `.lo = 0` (`presolve.gms:120`), the default `s29_fallow_max = 0` forces `vm_fallow(j2) = 0` everywhere, identically, regardless of any fallow *target* setting.

module_29.md flags this as a corrected R58 finding (lines 387-406): **`s29_fallow_max` is the enabling switch for the fallow mechanism, not `s29_fallow_target`.** Setting only `s29_fallow_target > 0` while leaving `s29_fallow_max = 0` does **not** enable fallow land — it instead activates the separate soft-penalty constraint `q29_fallow_min` (see next section) with nothing to relieve it, silently adding an unbudgeted per-cell penalty cost to `q29_cost_cropland` from 2025 onward, with zero fallow ever materializing and no infeasibility or warning. To actually run a fallow scenario, the documentation states you must set **`s29_fallow_max > 0`** (normally alongside `s29_fallow_target > 0`).

## Scalar controlling the fallow target, and its scenario fader

The **fallow target** itself (as opposed to the maximum-share cap in (c)) is controlled by a separate scalar:

- **`s29_fallow_target`** — target fallow share of cropland. **Default: `0`** (0-1 range; `input.gms:30`, per `modules/module_29.md:936`).
- **Scenario fader**: **`i29_fallow_scenario_fader(t)`**, applied as (`presolve.gms`, per `modules/module_29.md:357-360`):

```gams
i29_fallow_target(t) = s29_fallow_target * i29_fallow_scenario_fader(t);
```

The fader ramps the effective target between `s29_fallow_scenario_start` (default year 2025) and `s29_fallow_scenario_target` (default year 2050), using the interpolation form selected by `s29_fader_functional_form` (default `2` = sigmoidal, via macro `m_sigmoid_time_interpol`; `1` = linear via `m_linear_time_interpol`), per module_29.md's "Algorithm 1: Scenario Fading" section (lines 615-640).

`i29_fallow_target(t)` in turn drives the **separate** soft-constraint equation `q29_fallow_min` (`equations.gms`, per `modules/module_29.md:342-367`):

```gams
q29_fallow_min(j2)$(sum(ct, i29_fallow_penalty(ct)) > 0)..
  v29_fallow_missing(j2) =g=
    vm_land(j2,"crop") * sum(ct, i29_fallow_target(ct)) - vm_fallow(j2);
```

— a penalty-based *minimum* target (active only when `i29_fallow_penalty(ct) > 0`, i.e. after the policy start year, at `s29_fallow_penalty` default 615 USD17MER/ha), which is mechanistically independent of the hard `q29_fallow_max` ceiling in (c). Both `s29_fallow_target` and `s29_fallow_max` default to `0`, so neither the soft penalty nor the hard cap is active by default, and — per the (c) warning — enabling one without the other is a documented footgun.

## How cropland enters Module 10's land balance

Module 29 does not allocate land independently — `vm_land(j,"crop")` is one slice of the single shared variable `vm_land(j,land)` that Module 10 (Land, realization `landmatrix_dec18`, its only realization) owns and constrains globally. Per `modules/module_10.md`:

**Land conservation** (`equations.gms:13-15`, module_10.md lines 36-48):

```gams
q10_land_area(j2)..
  sum(land, vm_land(j2,land)) =e=
  sum(land, pcm_land(j2,land));
```

This forces the sum across **all 7 land pools** (`crop`, `past`, `forestry`, `primforest`, `secdforest`, `urban`, `other` — module_10.md lines 151-161) in each cell `j` to equal the previous period's total — land is conserved, only converted between types. Because `vm_land(j2,"crop")` is pinned by Module 29's `q29_cropland` (part a) and bounded by `q29_avl_cropland` (part b) and the fallow/treecover sub-constraints (part c and its siblings), any cropland change must be matched, cell by cell, by an equal and opposite change summed across the other six land types.

That reallocation is tracked explicitly by Module 10's transition-matrix equations (module_10.md lines 52-71):

```gams
q10_transition_to(j2,land_to)..
  sum(land_from, vm_lu_transitions(j2,land_from,land_to)) =e=
  vm_land(j2,land_to);

q10_transition_from(j2,land_from)..
  sum(land_to, vm_lu_transitions(j2,land_from,land_to)) =e=
  pcm_land(j2,land_from);
```

With `land_to = "crop"`, `q10_transition_to` requires that the sum of inflows from every other land type (`vm_lu_transitions(j2,land_from,"crop")`, e.g. deforestation-to-cropland from `primforest`/`secdforest`, pasture conversion, etc.) equals the cropland area Module 29 just computed. Module 29's own notes file reinforces this (`modules/module_29_notes.md:9`): *"Cropland area is constrained by the land balance. Expanding cropland requires reducing pasture, forestry, or natural vegetation — this is a zero-sum game enforced by q10_land_area."* Module 29 also directly reads back `vm_lu_transitions(j2,"crop",land_snv)` in its own SNV-relocation equation `q29_land_snv_trans` (module_29.md lines 313-338), so the cropland-to-SNV transition flow is doubly constrained: once generically by Module 10's transition accounting, and once specifically by Module 29's relocation-target requirement.
